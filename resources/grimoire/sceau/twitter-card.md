# Twitter / X Card

The Twitter Card tab controls how your content appears when shared on Twitter / X. Twitter uses its own set of meta tags, separate from Open Graph, though many values fall back to your OG settings.

![The Twitter Card tab showing Twitter sharing fields](/grimoire-asset/sceau/twitter-card.png)

## Card Type

Determines the layout of the Twitter share card.

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Summary with Large Image** | Large image above the title and description — recommended for most content |
| **Summary** | Small square thumbnail to the left of the title — suited to text-heavy content without strong visuals |

**Summary with Large Image** is the default and recommended choice. It significantly increases engagement by making the share card more visually prominent in the feed.

The image used comes from the **Social Image** uploaded in the Open Graph tab. There is no separate image upload for Twitter.

## Twitter Title

The title shown in the Twitter share card. Falls back to the **OG Title**, then the **Meta Title** if left empty.

- **Maximum:** 70 characters

## Twitter Description

The description shown in the Twitter share card. Falls back to the **OG Description**, then the **Meta Description** if left empty.

- **Maximum:** 200 characters

## Twitter Site

Your organisation's Twitter / X handle, e.g. `@yourbrand`. This appears as attribution on the share card and helps build brand recognition on the platform.

Include the `@` symbol.

## Twitter Creator

The Twitter / X handle of the individual content creator or author, e.g. `@author`. Used on article-type content where you want to credit a specific person rather than the organisation.

Include the `@` symbol. Leave empty if the content is authored by the organisation rather than an individual.
